What is the program about?

The program explores frameworks from economics, cognitive science, philosophy, and more, combined with workshops and guest speakers.

Who is the program for?

University students or 18-23 year olds, primarily from Taiwan, interested in interdisciplinary inquiry.

Where will the program be held?

The Compass Fellowship Program will be hosted in Taipei at the 9Floor CoLiving Space near Wenshan District.

When will the programs be held?

There will be two runs of the program, for 5 days each.

  • First Run: Saturday, January 24 – Wednesday, January 28
  • Second Run: Sunday, February 1 – Thursday, February 5
Eligibility and who are we looking for?

The Compass Fellowship is for curious and ambitious university students interested in reflecting and exploring greater breadth and depth in understanding themselves and the world.

We accept university students (or those of age 18-23), particularly for those in Taiwan, accepting about 20 students for each cohort.

While the program won’t have any hard pre-requisites, the staff team tends to have careers in STEM, so comfort with mathematics is recommended, but not a requirement.

How much will it cost to attend the program?

We are interested in forming the best possible cohort. To this end, the program will be free to attend and includes meals and accommodation.

Need-based scholarships to cover the cost of travel are available.

I am not in Taiwan, can I still apply?

To compose an exceptional cohort with diverse backgrounds and views, we also accept international applicants.

How does the admission process work?

Our admissions will be run in two stages. First will be the written application.

Admissions will be running from mid November to early January on a rolling basis.

How is the Compass Fellowship funded?

The Compass Fellowship is generously funded by Emergent Ventures and SPARC.

Who are the people running this program?

We have gathered an exceptionally accomplished staff team including graduates of Harvard and MIT with wide ranging work and life experiences from academic research, venture capital, startups, and more.

You can learn more about us on the team page.

Why are you running this program?

The program is inspired by programs like SPARC, which has been operating since 2012.

We think the ability to model the world, introspect on one’s own experience, and implement one’s vision are all crucial, yet often neglected, skills in one’s education. There is much room for expressing interesting and effective means of honing these skills and we are particularly interested in how this could work in an East Asian context.

Will this program be run in English?

The program will be run in English. Though most staff members are proficient in Chinese. It is recommended that you are comfortable in an English speaking environment.

That said, if you are interested in applying, we strongly encourage you to do so, even if you might not be sure your English isn’t up to par! We are looking for participants with high willingness to participate, not perfect English abilities.
